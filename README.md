# Day 09 — Population Health & Disease Risk Analysis

## Problem statement
Which demographic and lifestyle factors most strongly predict chronic disease risk,
and how do we stratify a population by risk tier to target interventions efficiently?

## Dataset
- CDC BRFSS 2015 (Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System)
- 441,456 US adults surveyed on health conditions and lifestyle behaviours

## Key findings
- Disease prevalence increases significantly with age — peaks in 65-80 group
- High BP is the most prevalent condition across all demographics
- Diabetes + High BP is the most common disease co-occurrence
- Smokers and sedentary individuals show significantly higher disease rates
- Southern US states show consistently higher chronic disease burden

## Risk stratification model
Composite score combining existing conditions (diabetes, heart disease, high BP)
and lifestyle factors (smoking, physical inactivity).
Tiers: Low Risk · Medium Risk · High Risk

## Recommendations
1. Target high-risk individuals with personalised health coaching
2. Physical activity programme — highest ROI lifestyle intervention
3. Smoking cessation — significant heart disease risk reduction
4. Age-targeted screening — prioritise 50+ for diabetes and BP monitoring
